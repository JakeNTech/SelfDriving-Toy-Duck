For my A-level computer science project I decided to make a self driving duck..although thats not how this all started. Let me take you on a little journy!

The first idea I had was to make a AI-sign recognition, like all new cars have, and relay that to a used using text to speech and a simple tinkter GUI; the only issue was this was too simple, it could have been done with only 50 lines of code so after some digging arround that was thrown off the table.

A few days later, and with no drink involved, I thought "What about making a self-driving little tikes car? It uses the same priciples as the sign recognition and would also be a huge riot" Jummping on google saw me looking at little tikes cars for a fiver near our house; then it hit me, like a big wall, somehow I would need to fill in the gap, join the front wheels together and make some kind of steering setup and then find a way to power it...A golf cart motor costs ~£170 plus the cost of a car battery to power it and then a load-balanacing charger to well...charge it. The next idea was to make a self-driving RC car, that would be safer, cheaper and easier to get to school! It was a good idea but then...


It's worth pointing out at this point that our computer science class has a massive obbsession with ducks!
